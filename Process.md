#  Supply Chain Management Process for Machine Manufacturing Company

## Overview

- This document explains the complete end-to-end supply chain process followed in a machine manufacturing company. It covers material planning, procurement, logistics, inventory management, and final manufacturing execution. The process supports multiple projects and department-wise requirements.

---

## 1. Requirement Planning (Department Level)

Departments such as Production and Maintenance identify material requirements based on project needs.

Example:
Production department raises a request for 100 Kg Steel Rod for Project A.
Maintenance department raises a request for 2 Motors for Project B.

---

## 2. Purchase Requisition (PR)

A Purchase Requisition is created in the system to formally request materials.

Example:
PR1001 created for Steel Rod (Project A).
PR1002 created for Motor (Project B).

---

## 3. PR Approval

The PR is reviewed and approved by the respective department head or manager.

Example:
Production Manager approves PR1001.
Maintenance Head approves PR1002.

---

## 4. Request for Quotation (RFQ)

RFQs are sent to multiple vendors to collect pricing and delivery information.

Example:
RFQ sent to ABC Supplier for Steel Rod.
RFQ sent to XYZ Pvt Ltd for Motor.

---

## 5. Vendor Quotation

Vendors respond with quotations including price, lead time, and terms.

Example:
ABC Supplier quotes 50 per Kg for Steel Rod.
XYZ quotes 5000 per Motor.

---

## 6. Vendor Selection

Vendor is selected based on cost, quality, and delivery commitment.

Example:
ABC Supplier selected for Project A as lowest bidder.
XYZ selected for Project B.

---

## 7. Purchase Order (PO) Creation

A Purchase Order is created and issued to the selected vendor.

Example:
PO2001 created for Steel Rod (Project A).
PO2002 created for Motor (Project B).

---

## 8. PO Approval and Release

The PO is approved internally and sent to the vendor.

Example:
Purchase Head approves PO2001 and PO2002.
PO is shared with vendors via email.

---

## 9. Vendor Acceptance

Vendor confirms acceptance of the PO and delivery schedule.

Example:
ABC Supplier confirms delivery in 3 days.
XYZ confirms dispatch timeline.

---

## 10. Material Loading and ASN Generation

Vendor prepares material and generates an Advance Shipment Notice.

Example:
ASN3001 generated for Steel Rod with vehicle details.
ASN3002 generated for Motor.

---

## 11. Transportation Planning

Material is dispatched using vendor or company transport based on cost and urgency.

Example:
Steel Rod transported via vendor truck.
Motor transported using company vehicle.

---

## 12. Freight Optimization

Logistics team ensures optimal cost by improving truck utilization and reducing empty run.

Example:
Materials for Project A and Project B combined in one truck.
Return load arranged to avoid empty run.

---

## 13. Material Delivery

Material reaches the company warehouse or plant.

Example:
Steel Rod delivered to main warehouse.
Motor delivered to maintenance store.

---

## 14. Gate Entry

Security records vehicle entry details.

Example:
Vehicle number and entry time recorded at gate.
Driver details captured.

---

## 15. Goods Receipt Note (GRN)

GRN is created after verifying material quantity.

Example:
GRN4001 created for Steel Rod.
GRN4002 created for Motor.

---

## 16. Quality Inspection

Material is inspected for quality and defects.

Example:
Steel Rod approved without defects.
Motor approved after testing.

---

## 17. Inventory Update

Stock is updated in the warehouse system and assigned a bin location.

Example:
Steel Rod stored in bin A1-R1-B1.
Motor stored in bin A2-R2-B1.

---

## 18. Material Issue to Project

Material is issued to the respective project based on requirement.

Example:
Steel Rod issued to Project A.
Motor issued to Project B.

---

## 19. Manufacturing Process

Materials are used in machine fabrication and assembly.

Example:
Steel Rod used in machine frame fabrication.
Motor installed in assembly unit.

---

## 20. Project Completion

Final machine is assembled and ready for testing and dispatch.

Example:
Project A machine completed and sent for testing.
Project B machine assembled and ready for delivery.

---

## Key Concepts

* Supports multiple projects simultaneously
* Handles department-wise material requests
* Ensures full traceability from PR to manufacturing
* Integrates procurement, logistics, and inventory

---

## Key Performance Indicators

* Procurement cycle time
* Freight cost per kilometer
* Truck utilization percentage
* On-time delivery rate
* Inventory turnover ratio
* Material rejection rate

---

## Conclusion

This structured supply chain process ensures efficient procurement, optimized logistics, accurate inventory management, and timely manufacturing execution. It helps reduce cost, improve visibility, and maintain operational control across all stages of production.
